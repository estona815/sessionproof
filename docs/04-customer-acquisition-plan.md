# Customer acquisition plan

## First segment

Independent artists and producers preparing a single release with 3–8 contributors and notes spread across chat, email, and spreadsheets. The buyer must have authority to share the submitted records.

## Seven-day pilot recruitment

1. Publish the live landing, privacy/limitations page, exact pilot scope, manual price, and private intake route.
2. Invite 15 individually selected creators through existing consent-based relationships or public opt-in communities; no scraping, bulk DMs, fake urgency, or automated outreach.
3. Conduct five 20-minute discovery calls: current workflow, last credit error, existing tools, security concern, willingness to pay.
4. Offer at most five paid pilot slots with the same written scope and clear cancellation/refund terms.
5. Record application → consent → upload → successful run → review completion → payment → feedback as separate evidence events.

## Fourteen-day revenue plan

- Pilot hypothesis: USD 12 per one-song Release Credit Check; confirm scope and price before invoicing.
- Internal goals, not official minimums: 5 real users, 3 arms-length paid customers, 5 completed runs, 5 feedback responses, 2 testimonial consents.
- Stop or revise if fewer than 2 of 10 qualified interviews describe a painful pre-release credit problem, no independent customer pays after a completed demo, or handling time makes the pilot uneconomic.

## Funnel

Qualified visit → pilot application → consented scope call → private upload → successful analysis → human review → paid delivery → feedback → repeat release.

## Interview questions

1. Walk through the last release's credit handoff.
2. Which documents disagreed, and when was the disagreement found?
3. Who confirms final splits and display wording today?
4. What would make you refuse to upload source notes?
5. Which output would save time or prevent a release delay?
6. What would you pay for one release, if anything?
7. What must remain a human decision?

## Outreach template

> I am piloting SESSIONPROOF, a small release-operations service that links proposed music credits back to the exact notes, chats, and sheets you choose to submit. It flags split totals, missing contributors, and conflicting versions; it does not decide ownership or provide legal advice. Would a 15-minute demo be useful for an upcoming release? I will not ask for private files until scope, retention, and consent are clear.

Success is evidenced by independent use and payment—not message volume, signups from team members, or fabricated scarcity.
