# AI-native business narrative

SESSIONPROOF is a pre-release music-credit operations business for independent artists, producers, songwriters, vocalists, engineers, managers, and small labels. A release rarely begins with one clean database. Contribution facts accumulate in chats, studio notes, emails, invoices, draft credit sheets, and release metadata. The business turns the limited set of records a customer intentionally submits into a reviewable contributor ledger, flags what remains inconsistent or unsupported, and delivers an evidence-linked release pack.

AI performs bounded operating work every time a customer starts a review. An Intake Agent inventories the sources, fragments text into stable evidence units, and identifies missing inputs. A Credit Extraction Agent uses Gemini structured output to propose contributor names, roles, display wording, titles, percentage claims, ownership types, confirmation statements, and sample mentions. A Reconciliation Agent prioritizes duplicate identities and contradictory claims. A Follow-up Agent drafts the smallest clear question for each open item. A Release Operations Agent requests deterministic readiness recomputation and prepares the approved export. Each run records tools, artifacts, provider/model, schema, prompt and rule versions, latency, retries, usage, errors, and the human-approval state.

The AI does not control the consequential decisions. It cannot invent a missing percentage, total or repair shares, merge “Jules Han” with “J. Han,” decide who owns copyright, clear a sample, contact a contributor, or approve a release. Every accepted claim must reference an existing fragment and contain a quote found exactly in that fragment. Zod validates the model response; unsupported claims are discarded. Versioned code calculates integer basis-point totals, detects conflicts, counts acknowledgements, applies the readiness policy, checks confirmation expiry, and totals business records. People choose the source set, approve identity mappings, correct values, decide whether expert review is needed, authorize contact, and accept the final pack.

This operating split matters commercially. The customer is not buying a chatbot answer. They are buying a repeatable, auditable release-administration workflow that can make a messy handoff easier to review. The initial paid product is one Release Credit Check with a manual invoice. A USD 12 pilot price is a hypothesis to test, not an industry benchmark. A later team pilot may support multiple releases and recurring confirmations. Human music metadata or rights-administration specialists may receive clearly scoped unresolved cases in a future referral workflow, creating paid expert work without misrepresenting the AI as a lawyer or rights authority.

The business should be judged on real, arms-length evidence. As of July 22, 2026, SESSIONPROOF has zero verified external users, zero paid customers, zero revenue, zero costs, zero marketing spend, and zero production Gemini calls. The public operator dashboard shows those zeros and keeps the fictional GLASS CITY demo separate. Before submission, the team must deploy on Google Cloud, run Gemini in the deployed application, recruit consenting independent creators, record actual product runs, collect feedback, earn and document arms-length revenue, reconcile May–August revenue and costs, and preserve private provider/customer evidence. Team, family, self-payment, related entities, refunds, and pre-existing relationships must be reported separately.

Privacy is central because unreleased titles, messages, emails, contributor identities, split discussions, contracts, and payment evidence can all be sensitive. The production design uses Cloud Run, Firestore, private Cloud Storage, Vertex AI Gemini, Secret Manager, and redacted Cloud Logging. Uploads use allowlists, size limits, generated paths, hashes, private access, retention choices, and deletion. Confirmation links use high-entropy tokens, stored hashes, expiry, revocation, and minimum contributor-specific disclosure. Original files and personal data never enter the public repository. Customer awareness and separate testimonial/judge-sharing consent are recorded before evidence is shared.

SESSIONPROOF's economic opportunity is practical: help small creator businesses preserve professional contribution evidence and reduce the administrative friction of a release, while creating a structured place for qualified human specialists when automation should stop. Its sustainability must be proven through paid pilots, measured processing cost, repeat releases, and honest unit economics—not fabricated metrics or fixture performance.
